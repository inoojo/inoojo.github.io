Custom Hangboard

Finished hangboard mounted on C-channel frame

Engineered Climbing Training Tool

Designed and fabricated a structural hangboard with a minimum Factor of Safety of 5, featuring precision dovetail joints, ergonomic holds, and optimized for mounting on a 1-1/4″ x 5/8″ cold rolled C-channel frame.

Project Summary

This project engineered a custom climbing hangboard with a minimum Factor of Safety (FoS) of 5 under 63.5kg distributed load. Starting with maple wood stock, we developed a compact design with unique hold configurations, integrated dovetail joints, and precise mounting for a 1-1/4″ x 5/8″ x 14-gauge cold rolled C-channel frame. Through rigorous FEA validation and precision woodworking techniques, we achieved a final FoS of 5.4 while maintaining ergonomic functionality and aesthetic appeal.

Design & Engineering

Structural Design Philosophy

Our design prioritized compactness to eliminate wasted space while incorporating 18 unique hold configurations. Each hole depth and spacing was optimized for varying grip positions, with hold shapes designed to target specific muscle groups. The C-channel mounting interface required precise dimensional tolerances to ensure secure attachment without compromising structural integrity.

"Early prototypes revealed screws alone were insufficient for sustained loading. This led to implementing epoxy-reinforced dovetail joints analyzed through FEA to ensure a minimum FoS of 5 under dynamic loading conditions."

Initial design sketches and hold configuration studies

Material Selection & Joint Engineering

Hard maple (Acer saccharum) was selected for its optimal balance of density (42 lbs/ft³) and compressive strength (7,800 psi). Through iterative design, we developed dovetail joints that distributed stress along the wood grain direction for maximum load-bearing capacity. Finite Element Analysis guided joint geometry optimization to achieve the target safety factor.

FEA Results: Maximum von Mises stress of 1,280 psi at 140 lb load with minimum FoS of 5.4 at critical joints

Precision-cut dovetail joint under magnification

Precision Fabrication Process

The fabrication process combined traditional woodworking with modern techniques:

Material preparation and custom laser-cut routing jigs

1. Material Preparation: Rough maple stock processed through jointer/planer to achieve dimensional accuracy, then cut to precise lengths on tablesaw with ±0.5mm tolerance.
2. Hold Manufacturing: Laser-cut router jigs ensured consistent hole depths and profiles across all 18 unique hold configurations.
3. Joint Engineering: Custom table router jig created for precision dovetail joints, with 3mm radius fillets routed on all edges for comfort and stress reduction.
4. Finishing: Sanding progression from 120 to 400 grit followed by food-grade mineral oil application for grip enhancement.

Key Takeaways & Skills Utilized

This project demonstrated that material grain orientation is more critical than absolute thickness - joints aligned with wood's natural fibers withstood 40% greater loads than cross-grain configurations. The FEA-driven design process validated that epoxy-reinforced dovetails provide 300% greater joint strength versus traditional screw fasteners.

Engineering Insight: "The integration of traditional joinery with modern FEA analysis created a product that exceeds commercial safety standards while maintaining aesthetic craftsmanship rarely seen in training equipment."
