Custom Hangboard

Hangboard mounted on C-channel frame CAD

Engineered Climbing Training Tool

Designed and fabricated a structural hangboard with a minimum Factor of Safety of 5, featuring precision dovetail joints, ergonomic holds, and optimized for mounting on a 1-1/4″ x 5/8″ cold rolled C-channel frame.

Project Summary

This project involved the design and fabrication of a custom climbing hangboard intended for use at my workplace, Texas Inventionworks. Hangboards are essential for training finger strength in climbing, but many commercial models have ergonomic limitations—specifically, symmetric hold layouts that result in uneven hand spacing and inconsistent training stimuli. My goal was to eliminate this issue by designing a board with offset, ergonomically spaced holds while maximizing the variety of grip types and depths within a compact footprint.

The board was engineered from red oak and mounted to a 1-1/4″ x 5/8″ x 14-gauge cold rolled C-channel steel frame. It features dovetail joints for structural integrity and ease of assembly. I performed Finite Element Analysis (FEA) to validate strength and reliability, targeting a minimum Factor of Safety (FoS) of 5 under a 63.5 kg distributed load. The final design achieved a FoS of 5.4 and combines functional hold variety, aesthetic form, and structural confidence.

Design & Engineering

Structural Design Philosophy

My design prioritized compactness to eliminate wasted space while incorporating 18 unique hold configurations. I carefully optimized each hole’s depth and spacing to support varied grip positions, with each hold shape intentionally designed to target specific muscle groups.

Early prototypes showed that screw-mounted joints were the primary failure point, prompting a redesign using integrated dovetail joints. These were validated through FEA to ensure a minimum Factor of Safety of 5 under dynamic loading conditions.

Initial design sketches and hold configuration studies

Material Selection & Joint Engineering

Red oak was selected for its optimal balance of density (44 lbs/ft³) and compressive strength (7,000 psi). Through iterative design, I developed dovetail joints that aligned with the wood grain to maximize load-bearing capacity. Finite Element Analysis guided the joint geometry to meet the target Factor of Safety.

FEA Results: Maximum von Mises stress of 1,280 psi at 140 lb load with minimum FoS of 5.4 at critical joints

Precision Fabrication Process

The fabrication process combined traditional woodworking with modern techniques:

1. Material Preparation: Rough maple stock processed through jointer/planer to achieve dimensional accuracy, then cut to precise lengths on tablesaw with ±0.5mm tolerance.
2. Hold Manufacturing: Laser-cut router jigs ensured consistent hole depths and profiles across all 18 unique hold configurations.
3. Joint Engineering: Table router used for precision dovetail joints, with 3mm radius fillets routed on all edges for smoothness and stress reduction.
4. Finishing: Sanding progression from 120 to 400 grit.

Key Takeaways & Skills Utilized

This project showed that aligning joints with the wood grain significantly improved strength, with grain-aligned configurations handling up to 40% more load than cross-grain designs. FEA confirmed that the dovetail mounting system offered far greater reliability than traditional screw fasteners under expected loads.
