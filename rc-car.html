RC Car - Machine Elements Class

Competition-winning RC car with carbon fiber reinforced components

High-Performance RC Racer

Designed and manufactured a competition-winning RC car with a $50 budget, featuring carbon fiber reinforced 3D-printed components, laser-cut acrylic frame, and precision Ackermann steering system with optimized caster/camber angles.

Project Summary

This project engineered a high-performance RC car to compete in a machine elements class competition. With a strict $50 budget constraint, we developed a lightweight yet durable design featuring a laser-cut acrylic frame, carbon fiber reinforced 3D-printed components, and a precision Ackermann steering system with optimized caster/camber angles. The car achieved first place in the competition, demonstrating exceptional handling and speed due to its innovative steering implementation and optimized power-to-weight ratio.

Engineering & Design

Precision Steering System

The core innovation was our Ackermann steering geometry implementation with optimized caster and camber angles. Through precise calculations, we achieved a 3-wheel turning capability with minimal tire scrub. The steering system featured:

Our calculations showed a 23° inner wheel angle and 17° outer wheel angle at full lock, with 5° caster and -1° camber providing optimal stability and cornering performance.

Frame & Materials Engineering

The lightweight yet rigid frame was laser-cut from 3mm acrylic, providing an optimal balance of strength and weight. Critical components were 3D-printed with carbon fiber reinforced nylon using Markforged and Raise3D printers for maximum strength-to-weight ratio.

Laser-cut acrylic bottom plate

Material Innovation: Carbon fiber reinforced components were 40% lighter and 300% stronger than standard PLA prints, enabling more aggressive suspension tuning without weight penalties.

Key Takeaways & Skills Utilized

This project demonstrated that precision steering geometry has a greater impact on performance than raw power. The Ackermann implementation with optimized caster angles proved to be the decisive factor in our competition success. The strategic use of carbon fiber reinforced printing allowed us to create strong, lightweight components while staying within budget.

Engineering Insight: "The integration of traditional mechanical principles (Ackermann steering) with modern fabrication techniques (carbon fiber 3D printing) created a vehicle that outperformed more powerful but less sophisticated competitors."
